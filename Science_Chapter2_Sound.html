🌊 Sounds Are Made by Vibrations

Every single sound you hear is made by something vibrating — moving back and forth very quickly. When an object vibrates, it makes the air around it vibrate too. Those vibrations travel to your ears and you hear SOUND! 👂

👆 Press the buttons to see a string vibrate!

A quiet sound — small vibrations back and forth.

🎵 Vibration = Sound
Put your hand on your throat and hum — you can FEEL the vibrations! Every sound from a whisper to thunder is made by vibrating.
👁️ Can You See Vibrations?
Usually vibrations are too fast to see. But you can see guitar strings blur when plucked, and rice grains jump on a drum skin when you bang it!
🚀 No Sound in Space!
In outer space there is no air, so sound vibrations cannot travel at all. Space is completely silent — even a massive explosion makes no sound!
🧪 How Well Does Sound Travel? — Click each material!

Sound travels through solids, liquids and gases — but better through some than others. Tap each to find out!

🌬️
Gas (Air)
Slowest — particles far apart
Sound travels slowest through gases like air. The particles are spread far apart so vibrations take longer to pass. Sound travels at about 340 m/s through air — still fast, but slowest of the three!
💧
Liquid (Water)
Medium — particles closer together
Sound travels better through liquids. Particles are closer so vibrations pass along faster. Whales and dolphins communicate using sound underwater — sometimes hundreds of kilometres away!
🪨
Solid (Wood/Metal)
Fastest — particles packed tightly
Sound travels fastest and best through solids! Particles are packed tightly together so vibrations pass extremely quickly. Put your ear on a wooden desk and tap it — it sounds MUCH louder than tapping through air!
🏆 Remember: Sound travels BEST through solids, then liquids, then gases (air). Solids are best because their particles are closest together!
📢 Volume — How Loud is a Sound?

Volume is how loud or quiet a sound is. A sound is louder when vibrations are bigger. We measure volume in decibels (dB). Sounds over 85 dB can damage your ears! 👂

🎵 Pitch — High or Low?

Pitch describes whether a sound is high or low. Fast vibrations → high pitch. Slow vibrations → low pitch.

🐘 Elephants use INFRASOUND

Very low-pitched sounds humans cannot hear. Other elephants detect them up to 7.5 km away!

🦇 Bats use ULTRASOUND

Very high-pitched sounds humans cannot hear. Bats use echolocation to navigate and hunt in complete darkness!

📯 Simple Tube Trick
A tube can make sound louder without electricity! It focuses vibrations into a narrow path so they don't spread out and get lost. Old gramophones used a horn-shaped tube to amplify music!
📏 Distance Makes Sound Quieter
As sound travels further from its source, it spreads out in all directions and gets quieter. That's why a voice from far away sounds faint!
🔇 Making Sounds QUIETER — Muffling

Sometimes sounds are too loud — like traffic or construction. We can muffle sounds using special materials. Click each method!

🎧
Ear Defenders
★★★★★
Protection: Excellent
Ear defenders are worn by people working in very noisy places — construction sites, airports, factories. Thick pads absorb sound vibrations before they reach your eardrum. Always wear them near very loud sounds!
🏠
Carpets & Curtains
★★★★★
Muffles: Moderate
Soft materials absorb sound vibrations. Hard floors and bare walls REFLECT sound (making echoes), while carpets and curtains soak it up. That's why an empty room sounds echoey!
🧱
Insulated Walls
★★★★★
Protection: Good
Buildings sometimes have sound-absorbing foam or fibreglass packed between their walls. Recording studios have extremely thick insulated walls to keep sound in and outside noise out!
🚗
Car Silencer
★★★★★
Protection: Good
Cars, trucks and motorbikes have silencers on their exhausts. Without one, engines would be incredibly loud. The silencer uses chambers to cancel out sound waves before they escape into the air.
🔴 Noise Pollution

Too much loud noise is called noise pollution. Sources include traffic, airports and factories. It causes stress, disrupts sleep and can damage hearing. Muffling technology helps reduce its impact on our communities!

🔁 Tighter = Higher
Tightening a string (turning the peg) makes it vibrate faster → higher pitch. Loosening makes vibrations slower → lower pitch.
📏 Thicker = Lower
A thicker string vibrates more slowly → lower pitch. A thinner string vibrates faster → higher pitch.
✋ Shorter = Higher
Pressing a string down shortens it. A shorter string vibrates faster → higher pitch. This is how guitarists play different notes!
🎶 Changing Pitch — Wind Instruments (Bottle Simulator!)

Blowing across a bottle top makes the air column vibrate. The length of the air column changes the pitch. Tap each bottle!

🔑 The Key Pattern:
More water → shorter air column → faster vibrations → HIGHER pitch
Less water → longer air column → slower vibrations → LOWER pitch
