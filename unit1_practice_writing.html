✅ Model Answer
One freezing winter morning, Grasshopper arrived at Ant's door, shivering from head to tail.

"Please," begged Grasshopper, his voice quavering with cold, "could you spare just a little food? I am so hungry I can barely stand."

"You had all summer to prepare," snapped Ant, folding her arms firmly. "I warned you clearly, and you laughed at me."

"I know," faltered Grasshopper, hanging his tiny head in shame. "I was foolish. I promise I will never make that mistake again."

Ant was silent for a long moment. "Very well," she said quietly. "Come in — but next summer, you will work alongside me."

"I promise," whispered Grasshopper, stepping gratefully into the warmth.

📝 Notice: 4 synonyms (begged, snapped, faltered, whispered), correct speech punctuation throughout, each speaker on new line, characters stay in-character.

✅ Model Answer — Ant
Ant is an exceptionally hard-working and forward-thinking character whose determination is shown in every action she takes. The writer uses energetic, purposeful verbs to describe her movement: she "scurried back and forth" and was "toiling just a little harder", both of which suggest relentless, mechanical effort rather than ordinary work. Ant is like a machine that never switches off — she does not pause even to "admire the glorious day." This simile emphasises her complete dedication; while Grasshopper enjoys the present, Ant sacrifices comfort for security. Her intelligence is equally impressive: the modal verb "won't" in "Summer won't last forever" shows absolute certainty about the future — she does not guess or hope, she knows. Overall, Ant represents the virtues of responsibility and wisdom, and her reward at the end of the fable is entirely earned through her own choices, not luck.
📝 Features: clear point (hard-working, forward-thinking), two text references, simile ("like a machine"), modal verb analysis, concluding link to fable's message.

✅ Model Fable — The Proud Lion and the Wise Mouse
One golden afternoon on the shimmering African savannah, a magnificent lion lay dozing under an acacia tree. His mane gleamed like a copper crown, and he believed himself to be the greatest creature in all the land.

A tiny mouse scurried past and accidentally woke him.

"How dare you disturb me, you insignificant creature!" the lion roared. "I ought to crush you where you stand."

"Please spare me," squeaked the mouse, trembling like a leaf. "I will help you one day, I promise."

The lion guffawed. "You? Help ME?" But he let the tiny mouse go.

Weeks later, the lion fell into a hunter's net. He might roar all he liked — he could not free himself. Then the tiny mouse appeared and gnawed through the ropes with her sharp teeth.

"I laughed at you," said the lion quietly, "and I was wrong."

"And I thank you for sparing my life," replied the mouse. "We are even."

The moral: No act of kindness is ever too small. Even the mightiest may one day need the humblest.
📝 Features: simile ×2, modal verbs ×3 (ought to, might, could), alliteration (magnificent mane implied, wise mouse), dialogue punctuation correct throughout, past tense narrative, clear moral.
