🔵 Rounding to the Nearest Whole Number

Rounding makes numbers easier to work with! 🐘

Real Life Example: It's easier to say "an African elephant is about 3 metres tall" than "the average elephant is 3.3 metres tall"!

📏 The Rounding Rule

↓
If the tenths digit is 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4 → Round DOWN (keep the whole number)
↑
If the tenths digit is 5, 6, 7, 8 or 9 → Round UP (add 1 to the whole number)

🧠 Zara's Puzzle!

Zara is thinking of a decimal number less than 1.

👉 The hundredths digit is four more than the tenths digit.

👉 The tenths digit + hundredths digit = 10.

What number is Zara thinking of? 🤔

Tenths = 3, Hundredths = 7 (3 + 4 = 7, 3 + 7 = 10) → 0.37 🎉

✏️ Worked Examples

Example 1: Write 10 000 + 2 000 + 300 + 40 + 5 + 0.6 + 0.07 in digits and words.
Answer: 12 345.67 → "Twelve thousand, three hundred and forty-five point six seven"
Example 2: Round 3.9 m to the nearest whole number.
Answer: Tenths digit = 9 (≥5) → Round UP → 4 m
Example 3: What is 3.45 × 100?
Answer: Digits shift 2 places left → 345
