📈 What is a Linear Sequence?

A sequence is a list of numbers that follow a rule. In a linear sequence, you add or subtract the same amount each time — that's the term-to-term rule! 🚀

Key Words:
Term — each number in the sequence
Term-to-term rule — what you do to get from one term to the next
Linear sequence — the jump size stays the same every time
🥞 Pancake Example:
1 cup → 8 pancakes, 2 cups → 16 pancakes, 3 cups → 24 pancakes
The sequence 8, 16, 24, 32… has term-to-term rule: add 8

🌡️ Counting Through Zero — Negative Numbers!

Some sequences go below zero! Negative numbers are less than zero — we write a minus sign in front. 🥶

Example: Start at 40, subtract 9 each time:
40, 31, 22, 13, 4, −5, −14…
The sequence crosses zero and keeps going!
Example: Start at −15, add 11 each time:
−15, −4, 7, 18, 29…
The sequence crosses zero upward!

Try: Start = 40, Jump = −9 👇

🧠 Think Like a Mathematician!

Pierre counts back from 88 in steps of 8: 88, 80, 72, 64…

Will the number 1 ever appear? How can you tell without counting all the way? 🤔

All terms in Pierre's sequence are multiples of 8 (88 = 11×8, 80 = 10×8, etc.). The number 1 is not a multiple of 8, so it will never appear! ✅

🔷 Square Numbers

A square number is what you get when you multiply a number by itself! You can arrange that many dots into a perfect square. ⬛

Square Numbers from 1 to 100:

Pattern! Square numbers are made by adding consecutive odd numbers:
1 = 1
4 = 1 + 3
9 = 1 + 3 + 5
16 = 1 + 3 + 5 + 7 🌟

🔺 Triangular Numbers

A triangular number is what you get when you arrange dots into a triangle shape — each row has one more dot than the last! 🔺

Triangular numbers: 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21, 28, 36…
The rule: add 1, then add 2, then add 3… (the jump size grows each time!)

🔺 Pascal's Triangle

Each number in Pascal's Triangle is the sum of the two numbers above it. Can you spot the triangular numbers hidden inside? 🔍

Did you notice? The diagonal highlighted in the triangle gives:
1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21… — those are the triangular numbers! 🌟

🧠 Think Like a Mathematician!

15 × 15 = 225 (a 3-digit number). What is the smallest 2-digit number you can square to get a 3-digit number?

10 × 10 = 100 ✅ (the first 3-digit number!)
9 × 9 = 81 (still 2-digit, too small)
So the answer is 10! 🎉

🔑 Prime & Composite Numbers

A prime number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself. A composite number has more than two factors. 🏆

🔑 Prime Numbers
Exactly 2 factors (1 and itself)
Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13…
1 is NOT prime!
🧱 Composite Numbers
More than 2 factors
Examples: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12…
They can be broken up!
🌐 Real Life Prime Numbers! When you shop online, your credit card number is protected by a secret code based on huge prime numbers. Primes keep your information safe! 🛡️

🧠 Arun's Number Puzzle!

Arun says: "My number is between 20 and 25. It's odd. It's prime."

What is Arun's number? 🤔

Numbers between 20 and 25: 21, 22, 23, 24
Odd ones: 21, 23
21 = 3 × 7 (composite) ❌
23 has only factors 1 and 23 → PRIME! ✅
Arun's number is 23 🎉

A prime number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself. If you can divide it evenly by any other number, it is composite.
