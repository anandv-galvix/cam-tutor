📌 Before you read — What is a FABLE?

A fable is a short story where animals act like humans. Every fable teaches a moral — a life lesson. Aesop was a storyteller from Ancient Greece who wrote hundreds of fables over 2,500 years ago!

🌾 The Ant and the Grasshopper 🌾

One fine summer's day, deep in a meadow, a grasshopper was bouncing about, chirruping and singing without a care in the world. An ant bustled by, weighed down by the enormous ear of corn she was lugging to her nest.

Time and time again, the grasshopper watched the ant scurry back and forth gathering food — insects, flies, grains of wheat — anything she could find, never once stopping to admire the glorious day or relax in the rays.

The grasshopper found this difficult to fathom and teased her as she busied by, saying: "Take it easy there, Ant! Why are you working so hard? The day is long! Food is plentiful. Come and rest awhile and listen to my latest melody."

"As it happens, Grasshopper, I am storing up food for winter and you should be doing the same. Summer won't last forever, you know!" snapped the ant, shaking her head and rolling her eyes as she continued on her industrious way, if anything toiling just a little harder.

Grasshopper guffawed at the idea of working on such a day and hopped happily off into the sunset, singing and jigging all the way.

And summer didn't last. It never does. Winter came, bringing barren fare and frosty fields. Grasshopper's song stuck in his throat as he shivered without shelter or sustenance, gazing wistfully at the ants as they munched liberally from their stores of food, shaking their heads at him and offering him nothing.

"How foolish I have been!" he wailed — for only then did Grasshopper understand that he should have made provision for winter as Ant had said. 🍂

💡 The Moral of this Fable

"Make hay while the sun shines" — This means: work hard and prepare yourself when times are good, so you are ready when times become difficult.

🔑 Key Words to Know

Fable — a short story, usually with animal characters, that teaches a lesson.
Moral — the lesson or message of a fable; what we learn about how to behave.
Folklore — traditional stories and beliefs that have been passed down through generations.

✏️ Comprehension Questions
These are exam-style questions. Always use evidence from the text in your answers!

🏆 The PEE Method — Use this for every answer!

Point — make your main point clearly.
Evidence — quote or refer to something from the story.
Explain — explain HOW the evidence proves your point.

Example: Ant is hard-working (P) because the text says she was "scurry back and forth gathering food" (E), which shows she never stopped working even in the hot summer (E).

Question 1 2 marks
Why did Grasshopper tease Ant about working so hard? Use evidence from the text in your answer.
💡 Exam Tip: A 2-mark answer needs TWO separate points or one point with evidence. Write at least 2 sentences.
Think about what Grasshopper said to Ant. He mentions two things: how long the day is and how much food there is. Why did these things make him think Ant was wrong to work?
✅ Model Answer (2 marks)
Grasshopper teased Ant because he could not "fathom" (understand) why she was working so hard. He thought there was no need to work because "the day is long" and "food is plentiful", so he believed there was plenty of time to relax. Grasshopper did not think about the future, only the present moment.
📝 Notice: This answer gives a reason (couldn't understand) + evidence (quotes from text) + explanation (only thought about the present). That's PEE!
Question 2 3 marks
How does the writer show us that Ant disapproved of Grasshopper's behaviour? Use TWO examples from the text with explanations.
💡 Exam Tip: A 3-mark answer needs 2 separate points, each with a quote AND an explanation. Look at Ant's words AND her actions.
Look for: (1) How does Ant SPEAK to Grasshopper? Find the exact verb used. (2) What does Ant DO with her head and eyes? What do those actions mean?
✅ Model Answer (3 marks)
The writer shows Ant's disapproval through her words and actions. First, she "snapped" at Grasshopper, which suggests she was irritated and frustrated by his teasing — a gentle person would not snap. Second, she "shook her head and rolled her eyes", which are gestures that show she thought Grasshopper was being foolish and irresponsible. These actions together create a picture of Ant as someone who found Grasshopper's carefree attitude very annoying.
📝 Marks: 1 mark for each example with explanation (×2), plus 1 mark for the overall point about disapproval.
Question 3 4 marks
Explain what the moral of this fable is and whether you think it is a useful lesson for people today. Give reasons for your opinion.
💡 Exam Tip: A 4-mark answer needs a clear explanation of the moral + your opinion + at least TWO reasons with examples from real life. Write a full paragraph of 4–6 sentences.
Step 1: What is the moral? (work hard now, prepare for the future). Step 2: Do you agree it's useful? Step 3: Give 2 real-life examples where this lesson applies (e.g. studying for exams, saving money, eating healthily).
✅ Model Answer (4 marks)
The moral of this fable is that we should prepare for the future when times are good, rather than being lazy and wasting time. This is shown when Grasshopper cries "How foolish I have been!" — he only realises his mistake when it is too late. I believe this is still a very useful lesson today. For example, students who revise regularly throughout the year do better in exams than those who leave all their studying to the last minute. Similarly, people who save money for emergencies are not in trouble when something unexpected happens. The story teaches us that short-term enjoyment is not worth long-term suffering.
📝 Marks: 1 mark — identifies the moral clearly. 1 mark — evidence from text (Grasshopper's quote). 2 marks — two real-life examples with explanation. Well done if you included all four!

🌍 About Aesop & Enrichment
Learn more about the world behind the fable — history, science, and geography!

🏛️ Who was Aesop?

Aesop was a storyteller who lived in Ancient Greece around 620–564 BCE — that is over 2,500 years ago! No one is completely sure where he was born — his name comes from the Greek word "Aethiop" which means Ethiopia, so he may have come from Africa. Aesop is believed to have been a slave who used clever stories to share wisdom. His fables have been translated into almost every language on Earth and are still taught in schools today!

🔬 Science Connection: Real Ants & Grasshoppers

🐜 Real Ants

• Live almost anywhere
• Live in colonies (groups)
• Eat almost everything — omnivores
• Can survive for months to years
• Do they really store food? YES!

🦗 Real Grasshoppers

• Live in warm places
• Live alone (not in groups)
• Eat grasses and plants only
• Usually don't survive winter
• Does their "song" stop? YES!

🤔 Discussion Question

Does the science match the fable? In real life, ants DO store food and grasshoppers DON'T survive winter. Do you think Aesop knew about the real behaviour of these animals when he wrote the story? What does this tell us about how fables work?
