💬 The 5 Rules of Dialogue
Learn these rules and you'll never get speech punctuation wrong again!

📋 The 5 Golden Rules

1
Put speech marks " " BEFORE and AFTER the spoken words — they show exactly what was said out loud.
2
Start the FIRST WORD inside the speech marks with a CAPITAL LETTER.
3
Put a COMMA after the words that INTRODUCE the speech — the comma comes before the opening speech mark.
4
Put punctuation (comma, ! or ?) INSIDE the closing speech mark. NEVER put a full stop before the closing mark if the sentence continues.
5
Start a NEW LINE every time a DIFFERENT PERSON speaks.

🔍 Annotated Examples

Ant said, "Summer won't last forever!"
comma before speech mark (Rule 3) Capital S (Rule 2) speech marks (Rule 1) ! inside speech mark (Rule 4)
"Take it easy," said Grasshopper, "the day is long."
Capital T (Rule 2) comma inside speech mark (Rule 4) lowercase t — speech continues (Rule 2) full stop inside final mark (Rule 4)

❌ Common Mistakes — Don't Do These!

❌ "summer won't last forever," said ant.
✅ "Summer won't last forever," said Ant. (Capital S, capital A for the name)
❌ "Take it easy". said Grasshopper
✅ "Take it easy," said Grasshopper. (comma inside speech mark, not a full stop)
❌ "why are you working so hard?" Asked the grasshopper
✅ "Why are you working so hard?" asked the grasshopper. (lowercase 'asked' — not a new sentence)

Why does it matter?

The word you choose instead of "said" tells the reader HOW the character is feeling — without you having to explain it. It makes your writing much more interesting!

✍️ Write Dialogue
Use everything you've learned to write a proper conversation!

Your task

Write a conversation between Ant and Grasshopper AFTER the story ends — when Grasshopper comes to beg for food in winter. Use the writing frame below to help you!

✓ New line for each speaker ✓ Speech marks around spoken words ✓ Interesting synonym for said ✓ Stay in character!
Panel 1 — Set the scene (narrator)
One freezing winter's morning, Grasshopper arrived at Ant's door...
Panel 2 — Grasshopper speaks first (he is cold and desperate)
"____________," begged Grasshopper, shivering in the cold.
Panel 3 — Ant replies (she is serious and disapproving)
"____________," _____ Ant.
Panel 4 — Grasshopper responds (he is ashamed)
"____________," _____ Grasshopper.
Panel 5 — The narrator's final line
Only then did Grasshopper understand...
⭐ Model Answer
One freezing winter's morning, Grasshopper arrived at Ant's door. He was thin and pale, and his teeth chattered like tiny drums.

"Please," begged Grasshopper, shivering in the cold, "just a few scraps of food — anything at all!"

"I warned you," snapped Ant, crossing her arms firmly, "that summer would not last forever."

"I know," wailed Grasshopper, hanging his head low, "and I am deeply, truly sorry that I laughed at you."

Only then did Grasshopper understand that music fills your heart but hard work fills your belly — and that it is wise to think of tomorrow, even on the sunniest of days.
✏️ Notice: new line for each speaker, speech marks used correctly, interesting synonyms (begged, snapped, wailed), punctuation inside speech marks.
🧠 Comprehension Practice
Answer these exam-style questions. Always look back at the rules you've learned!
2 marks
Q1. Explain TWO rules for punctuating direct speech. Give an example of each from the story.
Think about WHERE the speech marks go and WHERE the punctuation (like ! or ,) goes.
⭐ Model Answer (2 marks)
Rule 1 — Speech marks go around the spoken words: "Summer won't last forever!" — the speech marks show exactly what Ant said aloud.

Rule 2 — Punctuation goes INSIDE the closing speech mark: the exclamation mark in "forever!" sits inside the final speech mark, not outside it.
3 marks
Q2. Look at these synonyms for 'said': snapped, guffawed, wailed. For each one, explain what it tells the reader about the character's mood and what it adds to the story.
For each word, ask: what emotion is the character feeling? How does this word show it WITHOUT telling us directly?
⭐ Model Answer (3 marks)
Snapped suggests Ant was sharp and irritated when replying to Grasshopper — it shows her frustration without needing to explain it.

Guffawed shows Grasshopper laughed loudly and mockingly at the idea of working in summer — it makes him seem carefree and dismissive.

Wailed expresses Grasshopper's deep misery in winter — the long vowel sound echoes the drawn-out sadness.

Together, these verbs make the characters' personalities vivid without using extra words.
3 marks
Q3. Write a two-line conversation between Ant and Grasshopper using correct speech punctuation. Use a different synonym for 'said' in each line. Then label the punctuation features you have used.
Line 1: Ant speaks — choose a word like 'declared' or 'snapped'. Line 2: Grasshopper replies — try 'wailed' or 'faltered'. Remember: comma inside speech mark, capital letter first word!
⭐ Model Answer (3 marks)
"I warned you that summer would end," declared Ant, shaking her head firmly.
"I know," wailed Grasshopper, "and how I wish I had listened to you!"
