📚 What is a Fable?

  • 🐾 A short story with animal characters that act like humans
  • 💡 Animals have human personalities — called anthropomorphism
  • 📝 Always has a moral — a lesson about how to behave
  • 🌍 Found in many cultures — Aesop lived in ancient Greece!
🤔 Did you know?

Aesop lived in Greece in the 6th century BCE — that's over 2,500 years ago! His name may come from the Greek word "Aethiop", meaning Ethiopia. His fables are still read all over the world today! 🌍

🌾 The Ant and the Grasshopper 🌾

One fine summer's day, deep in a meadow, a grasshopper was bouncing about, chirruping and singing without a care in the world. An ant bustled by, weighed down by the enormous ear of corn she was lugging to her nest.

Time and time again, the grasshopper watched the ant scurry back and forth gathering food — insects, flies, grains of wheat — anything she could find, never once stopping to admire the glorious day.

The grasshopper found this difficult to fathom and teased her, saying: "Take it easy there, Ant! Why are you working so hard? The day is long! Food is plentiful. Come and rest awhile!"

"As it happens, Grasshopper, I am storing up food for winter and you should be doing the same. Summer won't last forever, you know!" snapped the ant, shaking her head as she continued on her industrious way.

Grasshopper guffawed at the idea of working on such a day and hopped happily off into the sunset, singing and jigging all the way.

And summer didn't last. It never does. Winter came, bringing barren fields and frost. Grasshopper's song stuck in his throat as he shivered without shelter or sustenance, gazing wistfully at the ants as they munched from their stores of food.

"How foolish I have been!" he wailed — for only then did Grasshopper understand that he should have made provision for winter as Ant had said. 🍂

💬 The Moral of the Story

"Make hay while the sun shines!" — Work hard when times are good, so you are ready for harder times ahead.

✨ Auntie Anthea & Gentle Geoffrey

This is a modern twist on the same fable! Summer had been a blast and Gentle Geoffrey (the grasshopper 🦗) loved every minute. His music was sweeter than ever. Auntie Anthea (the ant 🐜) had also enjoyed the summer — Geoffrey's music made her work feel easier!

When winter came, Geoffrey was famished and frail. He had no food or shelter. But Anthea had a sneaky plan — she wanted to invite him home... as her dinner! 😱

But clever Geoffrey started singing so beautifully that Anthea couldn't bring herself to eat him. Instead, he sang for his supper — and they both got what they needed! 🎵🍽️

🔄 The Twist!

In the original: Ant ignores Grasshopper. In this version: Anthea wants to eat Geoffrey — but his music saves him! The lesson changes too: "One good turn deserves another."

🔑 Modal Verbs
Modal verbs show how possible, necessary, or allowed something is!

🔑 Modal Verbs to Know

These are the main modal verbs: can, could, may, might, should, would, will, must, ought to, shall

  • must / will = very certain 🟢
  • should / ought to = a good idea 🟡
  • may / might / could = possible but not sure 🔵
  • can = ability 💪

📝 Negative Modal Verbs

You can make modal verbs negative by adding "not" or a contraction:

  • will → won't
  • should → shouldn't
  • can → can't
  • must → mustn't

🌟 Proverbs!
A proverb is a short, wise saying that teaches a lesson — just like a fable, but in one sentence!

🌟 What is a Proverb?

Proverbs use figurative language — they don't mean exactly what they say. You have to think about the deeper lesson!

Example: "Make hay while the sun shines" means: Work hard when times are good! (Not actually about hay! 🌾)

People like spending time with others who are like them.
Work hard and be prepared while you have the chance.
🎭 Literal or Figurative?
Some expressions mean exactly what they say (literal). Others use imaginative language to mean something different (figurative)!

🎭 The Difference

Literal = means exactly what it says 👉 "The ant carried the corn" (she really did!)

Figurative = means something different, more imaginative 👉 "Grasshopper had his head in the clouds" (he was daydreaming — not literally in the sky! ☁️)

📋 Features of a Fable — Remember These!

  • 🐾 Animal characters who act like humans
  • 💬 Dialogue — the characters talk to each other
  • 📝 A clear lesson or moral at the end
  • 🌈 Figurative language or alliteration
  • 📏 Short and simple story

🐾 Step 1: Choose Your Characters

Pick two animals. What personality will each one have?

⚙️ Step 2: The Problem

💬 Step 3: Write the Dialogue

What do your characters say to each other? Remember speech marks!

Speech Marks Rules 💬

  • Put " " around what is spoken
  • Capital letter after the opening speech mark
  • Comma or ! or ? before the closing mark
  • New line when a new person speaks
