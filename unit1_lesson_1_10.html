🌏 About This Story

This fable comes from the PHILIPPINES — an island nation in South East Asia. It was collected by Mabel Cook Cole in 1916. It shows that fables are found in EVERY culture around the world, not just Ancient Greece!

The Man with the Coconuts

One day a man who had been to gather his coconuts laden💡 laden = heavily loaded; weighed down with things his horse heavily with the fruit. On the way home, he met a boy whom he asked how long it would take to reach the house.

"If you go slowly," said the boy, looking at the load on the horse, "you will arrive very soon; but if you go fast, it will take you all day."

The man could not believe this strange speech, so he hurried his horse. But the coconuts fell off and he had to stop to pick them up. Then he hurried his horse all the more to make up for lost time, but the coconuts fell off again.

Many times he did this, and it was night when he reached home.

— Mabel Cook Cole, 1916

📋 Model Answers

📍 Setting: A road in the Philippines; the man is travelling home with his horse loaded with coconuts.

👥 Characters: The man — impatient and disbelieving; the boy — wise and observant. The horse carries the coconuts but is just an animal (not a character who acts like a human).

⚠️ Problem: The man ignores the boy's advice and hurries his horse; the coconuts keep falling off and he must stop to pick them up every time.

✅ Resolution: He arrives home at night — much later than he would have if he had gone slowly, exactly as the boy predicted.

💡 Lesson: Rushing causes more problems than it solves; patience saves time in the long run.

📜 Proverb: "More haste, less speed" or "Slow and steady wins the race."

🔍 Is This a Fable?
Not all short moral stories are fables. Let's check if The Man with the Coconuts qualifies!

🧐 What makes a FABLE?

A fable has SPECIFIC features. Click YES or NO for each feature below to check whether this story qualifies. Then see the verdict!

Does it have animal characters who act like humans?
Does it have a clear moral / lesson?
Is it a short, simple story?
Does it use strong figurative language or alliteration?
Does the main character learn something?
Does it come from a folklore / oral tradition?
⚖️ The Verdict

Although The Man with the Coconuts has SOME fable features (a moral, brevity, folklore tradition), it is UNUSUAL because the main character is a HUMAN, not an animal. This makes it more of a FOLK TALE or PARABLE than a strict fable. Aesop's fables almost always use animals. However, the moral lesson still qualifies it as fable-like.

✏️ Write an Analytical Paragraph
Explain whether you would classify this story as a fable. Give reasons and use evidence!

📐 TOPIC SENTENCE Structure

Start with: "The story [title] can/cannot be considered a fable because..."
Then: add evidence → give your explanation → write your conclusion.

📋 Writing Frame

Topic Sentence:
"The story 'The Man with the Coconuts' [can/cannot] be considered a fable because..."
Evidence 1:
"One fable feature present is... For example,..."
Evidence 2 (contrast):
"However, unlike a typical fable,... This is because..."
Conclusion:
"Therefore, this story is [best described as...] because..."

📝 Model Answer
"The story 'The Man with the Coconuts' can be considered a fable-like story, though it does not fully meet all the criteria. One fable feature present is a clear moral lesson: the story teaches us that rushing leads to more delay, not less — similar to the lesson in The Ant and the Grasshopper. Furthermore, it comes from the folklore tradition of the Philippines and was passed down orally. However, unlike a typical Aesop fable, the main character is a human man, not an animal acting like a human. In Aesop's fables, animal characters are essential. Therefore, this story is best described as a folk tale or parable, which shares the purpose of a fable (teaching a lesson) but lacks the defining feature of anthropomorphised animal characters."

🌏 Geography & Culture
Stories come from places — and understanding where helps you understand the story!
🇵🇭
The Philippines
🗺️
Located in South East Asia, made up of over 7,600 islands in the Pacific Ocean.
🏙️
Capital: Manila — one of the most densely populated cities in the world.
💬
Languages: Filipino (Tagalog) and English — both are official languages!
🥥
Coconuts are one of the most important crops — used for food, coconut oil, coconut water, and making materials like rope and baskets. This makes the story feel very REAL and LOCAL.
📖
The Philippines has a rich storytelling tradition — many folk tales, riddles, and proverbs have been passed down through generations.

💡 Where in the World?

The Philippines is east of Vietnam, south of China, and north of Indonesia. It sits in the Pacific Ocean. Because it is an island nation with a hot climate, coconuts grow abundantly — so the story of a man carrying coconuts home would be completely normal and relatable to Filipino readers!
