📋 Quick Facts — Parts of a Flower
🌸 Petals
Brightly coloured to attract insects like bees and butterflies to the flower.
🟢 Sepals
Small green leaves that protect the flower bud before it opens.
⚡ Stamen
The male part of the flower. Made of the anther and filament.
🔵 Carpel
The female part of the flower. Made of stigma, style and ovary.
🟡 Anther
Sits on top of the filament. Makes a yellow powder called pollen.
📏 Filament
The thin stalk that holds the anther up so pollen can be collected by insects.
✨ Stigma
The sticky tip at the top of the carpel. It catches pollen brought by insects.
🥚 Ovary
Found at the base of the carpel. Contains tiny eggs that become seeds.
🌱 What is Germination?

Seeds might look dead — but they are not! Inside every seed is a tiny plant waiting to grow. When the conditions are right, the seed starts to grow. We call this germination. 🌱

💧
Water
The seed must absorb water to swell up and start growing.
☀️
Warmth
Not too hot, not too cold — seeds need the right temperature!
💨
Air
Seeds need oxygen from the air to grow. No air = no germination!
🌑
No Light Needed
Seeds do NOT need light to germinate — they can start in the dark!

🫘 The Dry Seed

A seed looks small and dry — but there is a tiny plant hiding inside! The seed also has a food store to give the plant energy when it starts to grow. Seeds can wait for YEARS until the conditions are just right. The oldest seed ever to germinate was 1,300 years old — a lotus flower found at the bottom of a lake in China! 🪷

⚡ After germination, the seed shrivels and becomes small — because the plant has used up its food store!

🔬 Fair Test: What do seeds need?

Scientists test one thing at a time to find out what seeds really need. This is called a fair test.

🧪 Test A: Water
Put seeds in moist soil vs dry soil. Keep everything else the same. Result: Seeds in moist soil germinate. Seeds in dry soil do NOT!
🌡️ Test B: Temperature
Put seeds in a warm place vs cold place. Keep water the same. Result: Seeds in a warm place germinate faster. Seeds in a cold place are very slow or don't germinate!

🌬️ How are Seeds Spread?
🌬️ Wind
Light seeds with wings or parachutes float away (dandelion, maple). Wind-pollinated flowers are small and not brightly coloured.
🐦 Animals
Colourful, juicy fruits attract animals. Seeds pass through their bodies or hook onto fur/clothes (burdock, strawberry).
🌊 Water
Spongy fruits float and are carried away (coconut). Mangrove seeds float upright in the sea until washed ashore.
💥 Explosion
Some pods dry out and EXPLODE, shooting seeds out (bean pods). Others drop and roll along the ground (apples).

🐝 How Pollination Works

Pollination is when pollen moves from the anther of one flower to the stigma of another. Pick a method to watch it happen!

Insect pollination: A bee visits Flower A to collect sweet nectar. Pollen from the anther sticks to its furry body. When the bee visits Flower B, the pollen rubs off onto the sticky stigma — pollination complete! 🐝
🔬 Inside the Flower: The Pollen Tube Journey

After pollen lands on the stigma, something incredible happens inside the flower. Follow each step:

Step 1: Pollen lands on the stigma

A pollen grain arrives — carried by a bee or the wind. It lands on the sticky stigma at the top of the carpel. The pollen sticks because the stigma is coated in a special glue-like substance!
