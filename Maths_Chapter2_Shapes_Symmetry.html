🧩 Can Triangles Tessellate?

Tessellation means shapes fit together with no gaps and no overlaps! 🧱

✅ All triangles can tessellate!
Fun Fact! 🌟
Every type of triangle — equilateral, isosceles AND scalene — can tessellate. You can always fit triangles together without gaps!

🧠 Think Like a Mathematician!

Challenge: Can a triangle have TWO right angles? 🤔

Remember: all three angles in a triangle add up to 180°. One right angle = 90°. Two right angles = 180°... but there'd be nothing left for the third angle! So the answer is NO — it's impossible! 🎉

Scalene ✓ can have a right angle Isosceles ✓ can have a right angle Equilateral ✗ cannot have a right angle

🔺 Which Triangles Have Which Angles?

🟢 Equilateral Triangle: All 3 angles are 60° — all ACUTE! Can never have a right or obtuse angle.
🟠 Isosceles Triangle: Can be all-acute, or have ONE obtuse angle, or ONE right angle (then it's a right-isosceles triangle!).
🟡 Scalene Triangle: Can have acute, obtuse, OR right angles. The most flexible triangle!

🪞 What is Symmetry?

A line of symmetry divides a shape into two halves that are mirror images of each other! 🦋

If you fold along the line, both halves match perfectly.

🔍 Mirror Reflections

When a pattern is reflected, each point moves to the other side of the mirror line — the same distance away! 🏃

Reflection in a vertical mirror line
Remember the rules! 📏
• Each point reflects to the same distance on the other side
• The shape stays the same size
• Only the position changes — it flips!

📖 Worked Solutions

Question 1 — Triangle Types (3 marks)
a) All three sides = 5 cm → all sides are equal
Answer: Equilateral triangle ✓ (1 mark)
b) Two sides = 3 cm, one side = 5 cm → two sides are equal
Answer: Isosceles triangle ✓ (1 mark)
c) Sides are 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm → all sides are different
Answer: Scalene triangle ✓ (1 mark)
Question 2 — Lines of Symmetry (4 marks)
a) Equilateral triangle: 3 equal sides → fold along any of the 3 "height" lines → 3 matches
Answer: 3 lines of symmetry ✓ (1 mark)
b) Isosceles triangle: exactly 2 equal sides → only 1 fold works (down the middle between equal sides)
Answer: 1 line of symmetry ✓ (1 mark)
c) Scalene triangle: all sides different, all angles different → no fold works
Answer: 0 lines of symmetry ✓ (1 mark)
d) A square: fold vertically ✓, horizontally ✓, diagonally both ways ✓✓ → 4 folds work
Answer: 4 lines of symmetry ✓ (1 mark)
Question 3 — True or False (3 marks)
a) A scalene triangle can have a right angle.
A scalene triangle has all different angles. One of those different angles can be 90°, as long as the other two are different from each other and the total is 180°. (e.g. 90°, 60°, 30° = 180°)
Answer: TRUE ✓ (1 mark)
b) An equilateral triangle can have an obtuse angle.
An equilateral triangle always has angles of exactly 60°, 60°, 60°. An obtuse angle is more than 90°. 60° is not obtuse.
Answer: FALSE ✓ (1 mark)
c) All triangles can tessellate.
Every triangle — equilateral, isosceles and scalene — can tile a surface with no gaps or overlaps. You can always flip a triangle to fit next to itself.
Answer: TRUE ✓ (1 mark)
Question 4 — Draw/Describe Triangles (4 marks)
a) 0 lines of symmetry → needs to have all different sides and angles
Answer: A scalene triangle (e.g. sides 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm). No line can fold it so both halves match. ✓✓ (2 marks: 1 for triangle description, 1 for correct type name)
b) Exactly 1 line of symmetry → needs exactly 2 equal sides
Answer: An isosceles triangle (e.g. sides 5 cm, 5 cm, 3 cm). Only the fold down the middle between the two equal sides works. ✓✓ (2 marks)
Question 5 — Triangle with 60°, 60°, 60° (3 marks)
a) All three angles are equal (60° = 60° = 60°) → all angles equal means all sides are equal
Answer: Equilateral triangle ✓ (1 mark)
b) Equilateral triangles have 3 lines of symmetry (one from each vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side)
Answer: 3 lines of symmetry ✓ (1 mark)
c) Can it tessellate? All triangles can tessellate — you can fit equilateral triangles together by flipping them to fill the space with no gaps.
Answer: Yes, it can tessellate. All triangles tessellate. ✓ (1 mark)
Question 6 — Why Can't a Triangle Have Two Right Angles? (3 marks)
Rule: All angles in any triangle must add up to exactly 180°.
Working:
One right angle = 90°
Two right angles = 90° + 90° = 180°
But that uses up ALL of the 180° for just two angles!
The third angle would have to be: 180° − 180° = 0°
An angle of 0° is impossible — the triangle would just be a straight line, not a triangle at all.
Full answer: The angles in a triangle always add up to 180°. Two right angles = 90° + 90° = 180°. There would be no degrees left for the third angle (it would be 0°), so the shape cannot be a triangle. ✓✓✓ (3 marks: 1 for the rule, 1 for the calculation, 1 for a clear explanation)
