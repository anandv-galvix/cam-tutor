🧱 SOLID — Particles are packed tightly in a fixed pattern. They vibrate in place but cannot move past each other. Solids have a definite shape and volume. Examples: ice, rock, wood.

🌬️ What is Air Made Of?

Air is a mixture of gases. Here's what's in every breath you take! 🫁

Nitrogen 78%
O₂ 21%
CO₂+
🔵 Nitrogen ~78% 🔷 Oxygen ~21% 🟢 CO₂ ~0.04% 🟠 Water vapour & others ~1%
💡 About 4/5 of air is nitrogen and about 1/5 is oxygen. We need oxygen to breathe and stay alive! Nitrogen is used by plants to grow.

🏭 Air Pollution — What Pollutes Our Air?

🚗
Car & Vehicle Fumes
Burning petrol/diesel releases harmful gases like carbon monoxide.
🏭
Factory Smoke
Factories release sulphur dioxide and other chemicals.
🔥
Burning Waste
Open fires release toxic smoke particles into the air.
🤒
Health Effects
Breathing polluted air can cause asthma, coughs and lung damage.
🌿 Plants are heroes! They absorb carbon dioxide (CO₂) and release oxygen during photosynthesis — helping to keep our air clean. This is one reason why forests are so important!

🌟 Amazing Properties of Water!

❄️ Water EXPANDS when it freezes! This is very unusual — most substances get smaller (contract) when they cool down. Water actually gets bigger when it turns to ice. That's why ice cubes float in a glass of water!
🌊 Water is called the 'universal solvent' because more substances dissolve in water than in any other liquid. This makes it incredibly important for life on Earth!
💧 Water is the only substance that naturally exists as a solid, liquid AND gas on Earth's surface — as ice, water, and water vapour!

☀️ Step 1 — The Sun heats water: The sun shines on oceans, lakes, rivers and puddles. The heat energy from the sun warms the water at the surface, giving particles the energy they need to escape into the air as water vapour. Without the sun, the water cycle could not happen!

⚖️ Evaporation vs Condensation

💨 Evaporation

  • Liquid → Gas
  • Happens at the surface
  • Needs heat energy
  • Happens faster when: warm, windy, large surface area
  • Example: puddles drying up

💧 Condensation

  • Gas → Liquid
  • Happens when gas cools
  • Releases heat energy
  • Happens when: gas touches cold surface
  • Example: mist on a mirror, clouds forming

🔬 What happens to particles?

💨 During Evaporation:

Surface particles of the liquid gain heat energy. They move faster and faster until they have enough energy to escape from the liquid into the air as individual gas particles (water vapour). 🏃‍♂️💨

💧 During Condensation:

Gas particles lose heat energy when they touch a cold surface or rise to cold air. They slow down and come back together to form liquid droplets. This is how clouds and rain form! 🌧️

🪟 Try this at home! Breathe onto a mirror or cold window. The mist you see is condensation — your warm breath (water vapour) cools down on the cold glass and turns back into tiny liquid water droplets!

📚 Key Vocabulary

Evaporation
Liquid turning into gas at the surface
Condensation
Gas turning back into liquid when cooled
Precipitation
Rain, snow, sleet or hail falling from clouds
Water Vapour
Water in its gas form — invisible in the air
Water Cycle
The continuous movement of water on Earth
Surface Area
Bigger surface = faster evaporation!

✅ Soluble vs ❌ Insoluble

✅ SOLUBLE

Dissolves in water

  • 🧂 Salt
  • 🍬 Sugar
  • ☕ Coffee
  • 🫖 Tea
  • 🧃 Fruit juice powder

❌ INSOLUBLE

Does NOT dissolve in water

  • 🪨 Sand
  • 🌾 Chalk
  • 🟫 Soil
  • 🪵 Wood
  • 🫙 Plastic

📚 Solutions Vocabulary

Solute
The substance that dissolves (e.g. sugar)
Solvent
The liquid that dissolves (e.g. water)
Solution
Solute + solvent mixed together evenly
Soluble
Can dissolve in a solvent
Insoluble
Cannot dissolve in a solvent
Universal Solvent
Water — dissolves more things than anything else!
🧑‍🍳 Making a cup of tea is a perfect example of a solution! The tea leaves are the solute, the hot water is the solvent, and the cup of tea you drink is the solution! ☕
