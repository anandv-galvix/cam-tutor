🔍 Fact vs Opinion
Can you tell the difference? This is a very important skill for discussion and writing!

✅ FACT

Can be PROVEN to be true or false. Based on evidence, numbers, dates, things you can check.

Examples:
✅ "Aesop was a storyteller from Ancient Greece." (can be verified)
✅ "The ant stored food all summer." (stated in the text)

💭 OPINION

Someone's PERSONAL VIEW or JUDGEMENT. Different people may disagree.

Signal words: I think, I believe, it seems, in my opinion, probably, surely, arguably

💭 "Ant was too unkind to Grasshopper." (judgement)
💭 "The modern version is more entertaining." (personal view)

💡 Alternative Endings
The same story can have many different endings — and each one teaches a different lesson!

What is the oral tradition?

An oral tradition means stories are passed down by speaking — not writing. Because of this, the same story can have DIFFERENT VERSIONS and DIFFERENT ENDINGS depending on who is telling it and what they want to teach. Aesop's fables are a great example!

Alternative Ending A

"The Kind Ant"

When winter came, Grasshopper arrived at Ant's door, shivering and hungry. Ant looked at him for a long time. "Come in," she said at last. "But next summer, you will work alongside me." Grasshopper agreed, and from that day, they became the most unlikely but devoted friends.
💬 Discussion: What NEW moral does this ending teach?
Alternative Ending B

"The Clever Grasshopper"

Grasshopper shivered in the cold, but then had an idea. He gathered all the animals together and performed a concert so beautiful that they paid him with food. He survived the winter using the very talent Ant had dismissed.
💬 Discussion: What does this ending suggest about talent and being clever?

✏️ Your Opinion

Which ending do you prefer? Which teaches the most useful lesson? Give reasons!

⭐ Model Ideas
Ending A ("The Kind Ant"): teaches that kindness and second chances are more important than being "right". The moral shifts from "be prepared" to "be compassionate".

Ending B ("The Clever Grasshopper"): challenges the idea that only one type of preparation counts. Grasshopper's talent (music) turns out to be just as useful as Ant's hard work — just in a different way. This teaches us to value different skills.

Which is more useful today? Ending A teaches empathy; Ending B teaches creativity and resourcefulness. Both are valuable 21st-century skills!
📣 Opinion Paragraph Structure — PEEL
Use this structure every time you write an opinion paragraph and you'll always get top marks!

The PEEL Structure

P
Point — State your opinion clearly
Start by saying clearly what you think. Don't sit on the fence — take a clear position!
"I think Ant was right not to help Grasshopper..."
E
Evidence — Give a reason or example from the text
Back up your point with something from the story — a quote or a specific event!
"...because she had warned him multiple times that 'summer won't last forever'..."
E
Explain — Explain how it proves your point
Connect the evidence back to your point — what does this evidence show or prove?
"This shows that Ant gave Grasshopper every chance to prepare — he simply chose not to listen."
L
Link — Connect back to the question
End by bringing everything together and summing up your argument.
"Therefore, the consequences Grasshopper faced were entirely of his own making."

⭐ Worked Example (Full PEEL)

P I think Ant was right not to help Grasshopper E because she had warned him multiple times that "summer won't last forever" and he had laughed and ignored her. E This shows that Ant gave Grasshopper every chance to prepare — he simply chose not to listen. L Therefore, the consequences Grasshopper faced were entirely of his own making.

✏️ Now You Try — Write a PEEL Paragraph

Question: "Was Grasshopper right to enjoy summer instead of working?"

📚 Useful Words
I believein my opinionthis suggests furthermoreconsequentlyin conclusion arguablyit is clear thatthis showstherefore
⭐ Model PEEL Paragraph
P: I think Grasshopper was NOT right to spend the whole summer singing and playing, despite it being an understandable choice.
E: In the fable, Ant warned him clearly that "summer won't last forever", but he simply laughed and continued to dance.
E: This shows that Grasshopper had full knowledge of the risk — he was not ignorant, he was simply careless. His enjoyment came at the cost of his own survival.
L: Therefore, while it is human to want to enjoy the good times, it is also essential to plan ahead — and Grasshopper's choice to ignore this responsibility had serious consequences.
🗣️ Discussion Cards
Read the question, explore both sides, then give YOUR opinion with reasons!

How to discuss well

In a good discussion, you look at BOTH SIDES — not just the side you agree with. Say what you think, but also understand why others might think differently. Use evidence from the text to support your ideas.

1 "Was Ant right to refuse help to Grasshopper?"
✅ Arguments FOR (Ant was right)
Ant had worked hard all summer and earned her food. Grasshopper had been warned but chose to ignore the advice. It would be unfair to reward laziness. Helping might just encourage the same behaviour again next year.
❌ Arguments AGAINST (Ant was wrong)
Grasshopper would have suffered terribly. A compassionate person helps someone in need, regardless of past behaviour. Perhaps one act of kindness would have changed him completely.
2 "Is Grasshopper really 'foolish', or is he just different?"
✅ He is foolish
He was warned and chose to ignore the warning. He had no plan for winter. His carefree attitude nearly cost him his life — that is the definition of being foolish.
❌ He is just different
Grasshopper simply has different priorities — he values music, joy and the present moment over future planning. His singing made others happy in summer. Perhaps he and Ant just have different personalities.
3 "Which character would you rather have as a friend — Ant or Grasshopper? Why?"
Think about:
🐜 ANT is: hardworking, reliable, organised, serious, prepared, honest (maybe too harsh?)
🦗 GRASSHOPPER is: fun, carefree, creative, musical, joyful, present-minded (but unreliable?)
4 "Does the fable's lesson still apply today? Give a modern example."
The fable's lesson: "Work hard in the good times so you are prepared for the hard times."

Modern example: Students who revise regularly all term do better in exams than those who leave it all to the last minute (Ant = consistent reviser; Grasshopper = last-minute studier).
🧠 Comprehension Practice
Three exam-style questions. Use everything you've learned in this lesson!
1 mark
Q1. Is this statement a fact or an opinion? Explain your answer.
"Grasshopper was lazy and irresponsible."
Ask yourself: can you PROVE this is true? Or could someone else read the same story and disagree? Look for opinion signal words or judgement words.
⭐ Model Answer (1 mark)
This is an OPINION. Words like "lazy" and "irresponsible" are judgements about Grasshopper's character — different people might interpret his behaviour differently. For example, someone might argue he was simply carefree rather than lazy. There is no objective test that can prove he was "irresponsible" — it is a personal judgement.
3 marks
Q2. Write a paragraph giving your opinion on whether Ant should have helped Grasshopper. Use the PEEL structure and include evidence from the text.
Start: "I think Ant should/should not have helped Grasshopper because..." Then find a quote. Then explain what it proves. Then conclude.
⭐ Model Answer (3 marks)
I think Ant should NOT have helped Grasshopper, because she had clearly warned him that "summer won't last forever" and he had laughed and ignored her. [P + E]

This shows that Grasshopper made a conscious choice not to prepare, even with full knowledge of the risk — the consequences were therefore entirely self-inflicted. [E]

Furthermore, helping Grasshopper without any condition would have taught him that laziness has no consequences, which would simply encourage the same behaviour the following year. [E]

In conclusion, while Ant's refusal seems harsh, it is arguably the most effective way to teach a lasting lesson. [L]
3 marks
Q3. Explain TWO ways in which the discussion forum activity develops 21st-century skills. How does discussing opinions make you a better learner?
Think about what skills you USE when you discuss: do you think carefully? Do you listen? Do you organise your ideas? Name two skills and explain how discussions build them.
⭐ Model Answer (3 marks)
First, discussing different opinions develops CRITICAL THINKING — when you consider multiple views, you learn to weigh evidence rather than accept the first idea you encounter. This helps you become a more careful and independent thinker.

Second, preparing to present your ideas develops COMMUNICATION skills — you must organise your thoughts clearly so others can understand and evaluate them. Learning to back up your opinions with evidence is a skill used in every subject and in life beyond school.

Both skills are essential not just in English, but in every subject — and in every career and situation where you need to think, explain or persuade.
