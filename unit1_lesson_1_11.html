🗺️ Plan Your Fable
Great writers always plan before they write. Fill in all four boxes first!

📌 Three ways to create your fable

Option A: Keep Ant & Grasshopper but write a different ending
Option B: Use different animals but teach the same moral
Option C: Create a completely new fable with a different moral

🐾 Characters & Setting
Who are your animals? What personality does each have? Where does the story happen?
⚠️ The Problem
What choice does one character make? What goes wrong? What warning is given?
🌪️ What Happens
How does the problem develop? What are the consequences? What is the turning point?
💡 The Moral
What lesson does the reader learn? Write it like a proverb.

🏷️ Animal Stereotypes — choose animals that suit their character!

🦊 Fox = cunning, sly  ·  🐢 Tortoise = slow but steady  ·  🦁 Lion = proud, powerful
🐭 Mouse = small but clever  ·  🐺 Wolf = greedy, threatening  ·  🐦 Crow = vain
🦅 Eagle = wise, far-seeing  ·  🐇 Hare = fast but careless  ·  🐝 Bee = hard-working

🔑 What makes this a strong fable?

✅ Two animals with human qualities (anthropomorphism)
✅ Clear stereotypes: proud lion, clever mouse
✅ Dialogue correctly punctuated with synonyms for "said"
✅ Similes: "like a copper crown", "trembling like a leaf"
✅ Alliteration: "magnificent mane" implied, "wise mouse"
✅ 4 modal verbs: ought to, will, might, could, should
✅ Past tense narrative, present tense dialogue
✅ Clear moral stated at the end
✅ Both characters learn something — makes the story richer
